XJTaik

AI-Powered EAP Discussion Room

01. Motivation & Research

The Why

We chose the EAP student support track because our initial research revealed a critical pain point: most students experience high anxiety and "brain blanks" during English discussions, which prevents them from practicing before class and building confidence.


The Gap Analysis

Strengths: AI transcription, feedback, stable platforms.

Limitations: No real-time intervention, no progress control, no integrated system.


Stakeholders

Primary: EAP Students (anxious, avoid speaking).

Secondary: EAP Teachers (need participation insights).

02. User Requirements

3 Must-Have "Playful" Requirements

  • Low-pressure, judgment-free practice environment
  • User-controlled, gentle AI hints during silence
  • Supportive, clear feedback after discussion

Evidence: User interviews & field observations

03. Ideation & Alternatives

Crazy Eights

Design Alternatives

1. AI-driven real-time hint system (adjustable frequency)
2. Peer feedback mechanism with anonymous comments
3. Gamified participation points to encourage speaking
4. Visual discussion progress tracker for group tasks

Final Choice

We selected Alternative 1 (AI-driven real-time hint system) as the core function, combined with elements of Alternative 4 (visual progress tracker). This combination addresses the core pain point of student anxiety by providing gentle, user-controlled hints, while the progress tracker solves low-efficiency group work. It ensures psychological safety and meets all playful requirements.
